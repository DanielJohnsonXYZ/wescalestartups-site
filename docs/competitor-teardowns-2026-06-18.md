# Competitor teardowns — elements to adopt for WSS

Date: 2026-06-18 · Source: live homepages (ColdIQ, Growthmates, The Straw Hat Group, DoWhatWorks), reviewed for the [WSS User Journey Development] backlog item "review these websites and identify what elements I can take."

This is a "what to steal" doc — not a ranking. Each site is strong at something WSS is currently lighter on. Recommendations are mapped to existing WSS pages so they're actionable.

---

## 1. ColdIQ — coldiq.com
**What they are:** Done-for-you GTM agency (outbound + LinkedIn ads + content). Elite Clay partner.
**Hero:** "Tomorrow's GTM Systems Built for You Today."

**Strongest elements:**
- **A productized 90-day timeline as a homepage section.** "Your results timeline" walks through Week 1–2 (Define & Setup) → Week 3–4 (Create & Launch) → Month 2 (Amplify & Convert) → Month 3 (Learn & Scale) → Day 90 (Flywheel Performance Review), each with concrete deliverables. It makes an intangible service feel concrete and de-risked.
- **Risk reversal baked into the timeline:** *"If the results are strong, we move into a 6-month partnership. If not, you still leave with a complete playbook, assets, and a working system."* Removes the "what if it doesn't work" objection in the buyer's own words.
- **A genuinely enormous wall of named, quantified testimonials** — every quote carries a number ("77 leads a month", "$360k annualised in 90 days", "58 demos in two weeks") and a named person + company.
- **"Where we shine" stat cards** + partner-tier badge (social proof of capability, not just outcome).
- **Speed-to-market promise** ("first campaign live 2 weeks after signing") and **weekly live dashboards** (transparency as proof the system is real).

**Take for WSS:**
1. Add a **"What the first 90 days looks like" timeline** to `/how-it-works` or `/ai-driven-growth-sprint` style pages — phase-by-phase with deliverables, mirroring the AI Driven Growth Sprint offer in the theory doc.
2. Adopt the **explicit risk-reversal line** ("if it's not a fit, you still leave with the diagnostic, the bottleneck map, and the next 3 experiments") on booking/sprint CTAs — we already promise a no-pitch call; make the "you keep the artifacts" part explicit.
3. **Quantify every testimonial.** Audit current proof and ensure each carries a number + name + company, ColdIQ-style.

## 2. Growthmates — growthmates.club
**What they are:** PLG / activation advisory by Kate Syuma (ex-Miro Head of Growth Design).
**Hero:** "Grow meaningful products that delight your users." + "Get free playbook."

**Strongest elements:**
- **Sharp problem reframe, stated as the enemy:** *"Your product doesn't grow. Users leave right after first session. 40–60% of users leave before they feel any value. Most growth issues aren't marketing problems. They're Activation problems."* This is exactly the "it's not X, it's Y" move WSS uses with learning latency — done crisply with a stat.
- **Founder-as-asset positioning** — a full "Meet your Growth Partner" block with hard credentials (Miro Seed→Series C unicorn; worked with Grammarly, Manychat). Trust transfers from the person.
- **A real free-resource library** as ongoing lead capture: "25 Growth Flows", "Client Offers Template", "PLG Homepages", "AI Onboarding Flows", "100+ PLG Best Practices".
- **Productized audience / content partnerships** ("7.5k newsletter, 25k LinkedIn reach") sold as its own offer.
- **Pricing tiers with explicit deliverables** (course $1,199; sprint from €9,000; ongoing program) — and "pricing based on company stage."
- **Wall of love** with logos: Monday.com, Grammarly, Manychat, Mobbin, Zeffy ("+50% activation").

**Take for WSS:**
1. **Lead every cornerstone with a stat-backed enemy line** like Growthmates' 40–60% — our "learning latency" framing would hit harder with one hard number (e.g., share of activity that never becomes a decision). Worth sourcing a defensible stat.
2. **Build out `/resources` into a visible library**, not just a list — WSS already has the lead magnets; present them as a browsable shelf (Learning Latency Score, Growth Bottleneck Scorecard, templates), Growthmates-style.
3. **Strengthen the founder block.** Daniel's operator credentials (£18M+ influenced, exits, 479 sessions) should appear as a "Meet your growth partner" unit on key pages, mirroring how Kate's Miro pedigree anchors trust — ties directly to the founder-led-growth pillar.

## 3. The Straw Hat Group — thestrawhatgroup.com
**What they are:** B2B + public-sector content/AI distribution agency. Closest structural analogue to WSS.
**Hero:** "Experts in distribution: Social, Search, and AI." + "Built with AI, led by senior people, backed by a real studio."

**Strongest elements:**
- **A proprietary named methodology that's nearly identical in shape to ours:** the **Signal System** — Source → Stream → Amplify → Sense → Scale, "runs monthly without resetting… compounding through every subsequent month." This validates the Growth Signal Loop framing — and is a competitor we should differentiate from clearly.
- **Finite vs infinite game narrative** ("Most B2B marketing is still playing a finite game. Campaigns. Quarters. Reset. Repeat.") — same enemy as our "Random Acts of Growth," told as a story.
- **A free interactive diagnostic as the secondary CTA everywhere:** "Run the free LinkedIn diagnostic — a tool we built ourselves." (We now have two of these; they lead with theirs harder than we do.)
- **Numbered problem cards (01–05)** — "Recognised, but not remembered", "A disconnected demand motion", "Production without signal", "Invisible where buyers now search", "Your leadership team is your biggest untapped channel." Tight, buyer-recognisable.
- **Best-in-class FAQ** — answers "how are you different from a traditional agency?" head-on, defines their category terms, and is transparent about pricing (£2,999–5,999/mo, 6-month min) and location.
- **Strong call risk-reversal:** "Senior strategist on the call, not a sales rep. No pitch. No pressure." + "Call James directly on [number]. No middlemen."
- **Visible proof of capability:** studio photos, a scroll-stopping graphics gallery, embedded short-form clips.

**Take for WSS:**
1. **Differentiate the Growth Signal Loop from the Signal System explicitly.** They own "Signal System" for content distribution; we should make clear WSS is about the *whole* growth loop (positioning, experiments, assets, memory), not just content. Worth a comparison angle.
2. **Lead harder with the diagnostic.** Straw Hat puts "run the free tool" beside every primary CTA. We bury ours; promote the Learning Latency Score / Growth Bottleneck Scorecard as a persistent secondary CTA (the `/ai-driven-growth` wiring in PR #30 is the start).
3. **Adopt the FAQ-as-positioning pattern** — a homepage/services FAQ that answers "how are you different from an agency / a fractional hire?" directly. WSS has `/fractional-cmo-vs-agency`; surface that logic as on-page FAQs too.
4. **Use the finite/infinite-game story** as a narrative device for the Random Acts of Growth pillar.

## 4. DoWhatWorks — dowhatworks.io
**What they are:** Competitive A/B-test intelligence SaaS.
**Hero:** "Remove the guesswork from website optimization. We track millions of websites daily to find winning A/B tests from major brands."

**Strongest elements:**
- **Mechanism-as-proof:** a 3-step "how we see A/B tests from any brand" (crawl → analyze → validate) makes a black-box product legible and credible.
- **Interactive "explore by industry" selector** on the homepage — lets the visitor self-segment and see relevant value immediately.
- **Contrarian, data-backed content as the authority engine:** "The Problem with Social Proof — customer logos *lost* in most tests", "AI Messaging Loses in Tests (But Brands Use it Anyway)." Counterintuitive, citable, and perfect for GEO/AI-search.
- **BetScores** — a proprietary metric ("meta-analysis across 27,000+ A/B tests") that packages their data into a memorable, ownable IP.
- **Crisp "who is this for"** (Growth / Product / Designers) with a tailored line each.

**Take for WSS:**
1. **Publish contrarian, data-backed insight pieces** — the single highest-leverage idea here for GEO. "AI messaging loses in tests" is exactly the kind of citable, counterintuitive claim AI answers love. WSS's AI Discoverability and Random Acts of Growth pillars could carry findings like this (sourced from real experiment data where we have it).
2. **Name a proprietary metric.** The **Learning Latency Score** is already this — lean into it as ownable IP (like BetScores), reference it across content, give it a consistent visual.
3. **Add a self-segmentation element** ("who is this for?" / pick-your-stage) to a key page so visitors instantly see the relevant path — ties into the journey-map → site-structure work.
